## Outline CSEE talk
**20 April 2026**
### Context 
1. Climate change will impact forests in many ways, but there is uncertainty regarding the direction that forests' carbon source sink dynamics will take
2. A hopeful outcome was that with warming temperature, increased atm CO2 would be offset by increased carbon sequestration in forests. 
3. The proposed mechanism was by: a) carbon fertilization, suggesting tree growth is limited by atm CO2 concentrations and by the time provided in a year to growh--> growing season extension which are driven by phenology.
4. Research shows convincing evidence that warmer springs advanced the onset of the growing season shown by budbursts.
5. Autumn phenology is also delayed, but the patterns are not as clear
6. This lengthens the overall number of days during which trees can grow.  Growing season shifts with climate change also means better conditions for the trees, but also a shift of the temperature the trees experience, with growth onset means cooler and less predictable temperature in spring.
7. However, recent work has failed to find this expected relationship and suggest drought and competition as potential factors that would offset the expected growh increase with CC.
7. With my project, I aim to tackle this important question by leveraging the phenology data from a common garden study where drought and competition are absent
8. This project spans 3 years of data, 4 species, 4 sites and 227 single growing seasons to understand if a longer season increased growth for juvenile trees
9. For this, we fitted a 3-way bayesian hierarchical model as a function of thermal season, the length of the growing season (days), but also just as the SOS and EOS.
10. Starting with just the start and end of the GS, we found that growth increased with earlier spring for two species, but only for one species for the end of season.
11. Interestingly, the effect of SOS on BPOP disappeared when considering the whole season. calculated from leafout (sos) to budset (eos). However, for the other three species, growth increased with a longer GS, just when calculating it in days
12. Even more interesting, when we calculated the GDD within that exact period, the effect decreased and only one species showed a positive relationship with the thermal season.
